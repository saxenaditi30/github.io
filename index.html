Introduction

Durability is a mechanical problem
as much as an electrochemical one.

I'm a HITEC Fellow and doctoral researcher at Forschungszentrum Jülich (IMD-1), completing my PhD in collaboration with RWTH Aachen under Prof. Dr. Ruth Schwaiger. My research sits where materials mechanics meets electrochemical energy: understanding how PEM fuel cell membrane electrode assemblies fail under the coupled mechanical, chemical, and electrical stresses of real operation.

My thesis develops a multi-modal mechanical characterization framework combining nanoindentation, microscratch testing, and a custom electro-mechanical puncture methodology, supported by Python-based statistical pipelines including OLS regression, F-statistics, and Weibull maximum-likelihood estimation with right-censored data.

Research Projects

Selected Work

01

Nanoindentation of Catalyst Layers

Nanoindentation testing on CCS and CCM samples revealed that this loading regime does not induce detectable fracture in PEMFC catalyst layers. The mechanical response is governed by densification of the porous microstructure and viscoelastic deformation of the ionomer–membrane system, which suppresses the discrete signatures typically used to extract fracture properties.

Response dominated by microstructural densification — mechanical baseline established

02

Microscratch Testing for Interface Adhesion

Microscratch testing revealed crack formation and interfacial delamination, enabling a comparative assessment of adhesion between CCS and CCM samples. Critical delamination forces differed significantly between fabrication routes. Multivariate regression identified sample type as the dominant predictor of delamination force; scatter driven by microstructural heterogeneity currently limits fully quantitative throughput application.

Sample type identified as dominant predictor — qualitative insight established
Ramped loadOLS regressionSEMConfocal
03

Electro-Mechanical Puncture Test

A custom test combining needle indentation with in-situ electrical monitoring identified three failure regimes: mechanically dominated behavior at low voltage, a stochastic electro-mechanical transition at intermediate voltages, and electrically driven breakthrough at high voltage. Increasing electric field strength systematically reduced breakthrough stress, with membrane architecture and thickness influencing the overall response.

Three failure regimes identified — electric field dependence demonstrated
