Introduction

Durability is a mechanical problem
as much as an electrochemical one.

I'm a HITEC Fellow and PhD candidate at Forschungszentrum Jülich (IMD-1), completing my doctorate with RWTH Aachen. My research sits where materials mechanics meets electrochemical energy: understanding how PEM fuel cell membrane electrode assemblies fail under the coupled mechanical, chemical, and electrical stresses of real operation.

MEAs fail through three distinct mechanical pathways — bulk fatigue of the membrane, delamination at the catalyst layer–membrane interface, and localized puncture from foreign particles. Each demands a different probe. My thesis develops a multi-modal characterization framework that combines nanoindentation, nanoscratching, and a custom electro-mechanical puncture test to resolve degradation at the length scales — and under the hydrated, polarized conditions — where it actually originates.
