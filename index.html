Introduction

Durability is a mechanical problem
as much as an electrochemical one.

I'm a HITEC Fellow and doctoral researcher at Forschungszentrum Jülich (IMD-1), completing my PhD in collaboration with RWTH Aachen under Prof. Dr. Ruth Schwaiger. My research sits where materials mechanics meets electrochemical energy: understanding how PEM fuel cell membrane electrode assemblies fail under the coupled mechanical, chemical, and electrical stresses of real operation.

My thesis develops a multi-modal mechanical characterization framework combining nanoindentation, microscratch testing, and a custom electro-mechanical puncture methodology to resolve degradation at the length scales — and under the hydrated, polarized conditions — where it actually originates. This is supported by Python-based statistical pipelines including OLS regression, F-statistics, and Weibull maximum-likelihood estimation with right-censored data.

Professional Journey

Experience

Doctoral Researcher & HITEC Fellow — Forschungszentrum Jülich (IMD-1)

Sep 2022 – Present · Jülich, Germany · In collaboration with RWTH Aachen University
  • Developed an electro-mechanical puncture testing methodology for hydrated catalyst-coated membranes (CCMs), combining controlled needle indentation with in-situ electrical breakthrough detection to characterize pin-hole initiation under operationally relevant conditions.
  • Quantified critical loads for delamination and crack initiation across two CCM fabrication routes (doctor-blade-coated, screen-printed) using nanoindentation and microscratch testing.
  • Investigated mechanical strength of electrode–membrane interfaces under hydrated and non-hydrated conditions, elucidating the role of water uptake in interfacial mechanical response.
  • Implemented Python-based statistical analysis pipelines (OLS regression, F-statistics, Pearson correlation, Weibull MLE with right-censored run-out data); built automated workflows for cross-experiment comparison and visualization.
  • Performed post-mortem characterisation of failure sites using SEM and confocal laser scanning microscopy across multiple voltage conditions.
  • Presented at MSE 2024 (Darmstadt); two first-author manuscripts in preparation for peer-reviewed journals.
  • Mentored Master’s students in experimental methods, data analysis, and scientific writing.
